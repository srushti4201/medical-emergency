A medical emergency is a sudden and serious situation that requires immediate medical attention to prevent severe injury, permanent damage, or death. This can include heart attacks, strokes, breathing difficulties, or major trauma.

üöë When Should You Call for Help?

  • Difficulty breathing or choking
  • Severe chest pain or pressure
  • Uncontrolled bleeding
  • Severe burns or electric shock
  • Sudden weakness or paralysis
  • Seizures or unconsciousness

‚õëÔ∏è Basic First Aid Tips

Heart Attack: Call emergency services immediately and keep the person calm.

Choking: Perform the Heimlich maneuver if the person cannot breathe.

Bleeding: Apply firm pressure with a clean cloth to stop the bleeding.

Burns: Run cool water over the burn but do not use ice.

Seizures: Do not restrain the person, move objects away, and place them on their side.

üìö Common Medical Conditions & Emergency Handling

Stroke: Look for FAST signs - Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 108.

Asthma Attack: Help the person sit upright and use their inhaler.

Diabetic Emergency: Give them a sugary drink or food if conscious.

Fractures: Keep the limb still and apply a splint if possible.
